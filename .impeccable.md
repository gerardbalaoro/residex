# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

Medical residents logging clinical cases — primarily at home on desktop after shifts, occasionally on mobile or tablet while away. Not used during clinical encounters. The act of logging is a habitual, personal routine, like filling in a professional journal. Users are tired, time-constrained, and doing this out of necessity but ideally without dread.

### Brand Personality

**3 words**: Comfortable, practiced, human.

This is a personal logbook for clinical work — not a spreadsheet, not a database, not an analytics dashboard. The interface should feel like a well-designed notebook you reach for, not a system you're obligated to feed.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: Both light and dark, with equal intentionality. Light for daytime; dark for late-night logging after long shifts.
- **Tone**: Warm and unhurried. No harsh contrasts, alarm-state colors, or visual aggression. The emotional baseline is _trust_, not urgency.
- **Anti-references**: Dense data tables, cold enterprise grids, clinical analytics dashboards, anything that feels like surveillance or compliance reporting.
- **Typography feel**: Readable, unobtrusive, slightly warm. Not technical-monospace. Not editorial-serif. Something that reads like well-formatted text, not a form field.
- **Layout feel**: Document-like, not form-like. The case entry form should feel like filling in a well-designed record, not submitting a ticket.
- **Existing palette**: Teal primary (`#00786F`, `oklch(0.511 0.096 186.391)`) — keep. It reads as medical without being cold hospital blue. Neutrals should be warm-tinted toward the teal hue, not neutral gray.

### Design Principles

1. **Logbook, not database** — Layouts feel like a well-designed personal record, not enterprise data entry. Card-based or document-like, never pure tables.

2. **Friction-free entry** — Case form feels like a conversation, not an interrogation. Progressive disclosure, smart grouping, no overwhelming optionality upfront.

3. **Calm authority** — Confident without aggression. No alarm colors in normal states. Error and warning states are firm but gentle. The interface is never anxious.

4. **Earned density** — Case list is scannable and information-rich without being overwhelming. Form entry is spacious and focused. Density matches the task.

5. **Both-mode native** — Light and dark are equally intentional. Neither is a fallback or an afterthought.

### Priority Screens

- **Cases list**: Scannable, warm, card or document row style — not a spreadsheet grid.
- **Case entry form**: Progressive, comfortable, grouped logically by clinical context — not a flat field dump.
